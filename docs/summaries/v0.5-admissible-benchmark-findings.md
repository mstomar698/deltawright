## §10 assessment (on real third-party code)

**(a) token win — HOLDS on code we didn't write.**
- **Vs re-snapshot** (the MCP default): **0.19–0.45×** on every interaction — the delta is
  2–5× smaller than re-dumping the a11y tree, even on a small app.
- **Vs a STRUCTURE-AWARE diff at information parity** (the strongest fair incumbent —
  reorder-safe, minimal): delta-lite is **0.27–0.69×** on toggle/delete/filter-nav and
  **~tied (1.03)** on add. Honest: against a *good* diff of a tiny insert, the delta can't
  win much (both are ~30 tokens); its edge grows for removals (0.27) and attribute flips.
- The full delta additionally carries geometry + actionability the diff structurally cannot.

**(b) noise — not exercised here (by design).** TodoMVC has no background churn, so this
run isolates *author bias*, not noise. And the coalescer is **exact** on real third-party
DOM: reported `nodes` = 2–4 = precisely the change scope (new `<li>` + footer count;
removed `<li>` + count; the flipped item; the filtered-out items). No over-reporting. The
noise stress remains #23's synthetic finding → causal attribution (#15).

**(c) settle — PERFECT on the real quiet app.** `capped 0/30` on every interaction
(including the **navigation**) — settle quiesced cleanly every time.

**Recall AND precision — 30/30 on all four interactions, both frameworks.** The delta
captured the primary intended change in *every* trial (recall), including the hash-route
navigation (it reported the filtered-out items as removed) — and every node it reported was
an expected consequence (precision): zero over-report against the hand-labeled change set. It
never missed a change and never padded one.

**TIME — the one real, honest regression, confirmed on real UI.** The delta is
**~7–10× slower** than snapshot+diff on add/toggle/delete (1.4–1.7 s vs 0.14–0.22 s). Root
cause found: TodoMVC's `.destroy` button is `opacity:0` until hover, so it is *correctly*
NOT-actionable — but the trial-probe waits the full `trialTimeoutMs` (1200 ms) to determine
that. So a real hover-reveal control costs a full timeout. filter-nav is fast (215 ms)
because its changes are removals + already-actionable links (no slow probe). The verdict is
*right*; the cost is the O(nodes) serial trial with a full-timeout floor → async/bounded
reconciliation + a smarter not-actionable short-circuit (#18).

> **Update — #18 landed.** Reconciliation is now bounded-concurrent (each node still gets
> its full authoritative trial; verdicts + recall unchanged, 30/30). The reconcile step is
> ~8× faster on many-node deltas; on this small app the gain is minor (few nodes, one slow
> probe sets the floor), but on a 305-node delta the overall action dropped 12.3 s → 3.4 s.

**Overall: the author-bias objection is removed and the core bet HOLDS on real third-party
code** — a consistent token win over re-snapshot, competitive-to-better vs a structure-aware
diff at parity, exact coalescing, reliable settle, and perfect recall including on a
navigation. The single confirmed weakness is the actionability *time* cost, which real
hover-reveal UIs make concrete (#18). This is a materially stronger §10 signal than #23 —
though still one app, one framework, quiet, cl100k (not Claude). The remaining gaps are #25's
open items.

## Tier-2 — real live apps (2026-07-13)
The Tier-1 corpus is frozen + quiet by design. To check the story on **real production DOM with
forms + overlays**, `bench/run-live.ts` ran the same delta-vs-incumbent methodology against **two
real web apps** — a public **Next.js marketing site** and an authenticated **React application** —
on **Gemini's real deployment tokenizer** (`countTokens`). Targets + credentials are gitignored;
only sanitized metrics are recorded (no page content, URLs, or creds). **DIRECTIONAL: N=3, live
DOM is non-deterministic — the ratios are the robust signal, not the absolute counts.**

| app | interaction | delta | delta‑lite | struct‑diff | re‑snapshot | lite ÷ sd | delta ÷ re‑snap | nodes | not‑act | covered |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Next.js | content tab A | 357 | 148 | 406 | 2929 | **0.36** | **0.12** | 19 | 2 | 0 |
| Next.js | content tab B | 569 | 172 | 348 | 3091 | **0.49** | **0.18** | 22 | 5 | **3** |
| Next.js | mega‑menu overlay A | 69 | 34 | 144 | 3307 | **0.24** | **0.02** | 5 | 0 | 0 |
| Next.js | mega‑menu overlay B | 70 | 34 | 165 | 3329 | **0.21** | **0.02** | 5 | 0 | 0 |
| React (auth) | view‑switch | 1055 | 589 | 13138 | 470 | **0.04** | 2.24 | 59 | 3 | **2** |

**What holds (stronger than Tier-1):** (multiples below are computed from the raw counts, not by
inverting the rounded ratios)
- **Vs re‑snapshot on the Next.js site: 0.02–0.18× — the delta is ~5–48× smaller** than re‑dumping
  the a11y tree (its real tree is ~3000 tokens). Real-world DOM is big → exactly where the delta
  wins; far beyond TodoMVC's 0.18–0.56×.
- **Vs a structure‑aware diff: 0.04–0.49× (always <1).** The standout is the React **view‑switch
  at 0.04× — ~22× smaller** (13138 ÷ 589): a big view swap explodes the diff to ~13k tokens while
  the coalesced delta stays ~600 lite tokens.
- **Real overlays exercised** — the `covered` / `not‑actionable` columns are non‑zero on real
  menus + views (covered up to 3, NOT‑actionable up to 5): the covered‑target capability the frozen
  Tier‑1 corpus cannot exercise, now EXERCISED on modern React/Next DOM. Note: Tier‑2 **counts** the
  verdicts (they fire on real DOM) but does **not** ground‑truth their correctness — unlike Tier‑1's
  hand‑labeled precision/recall. **Settle never capped (0/3); no rep‑errors on the 5 reported rows.**

**The one HONEST limit this surfaced — the React view‑switch `delta ÷ re‑snap = 2.24`:** when an
action navigates to a much **smaller** page, re‑snapshotting that small destination (470 tok) is
*cheaper* than the delta (1055 tok) — because the delta additionally carries everything **removed**
(the large prior view) that a fresh snapshot silently drops. So the delta is **not** universally
smaller than re‑snapshot; it wins hugest vs a *diff* of a big transition (0.04×) and uniquely
reports what was removed + the actionability a snapshot lacks, but for a shrink‑navigation a fresh
snapshot of the small new page can be the cheaper *lossy* view. (One additional React interaction
errored on a selector — excluded; not a DW issue.)

**Net:** the token‑compactness bet **holds on real production apps** (measured on a real deployment
tokenizer), and the covered/actionability surface is **exercised** there (emitted on real DOM, not
yet ground‑truthed at Tier‑2) — with the honest caveat that "delta < re‑snapshot"
is not universal (it's about *what changed* vs a *lossy new‑state dump*; vs the fair *what‑changed*
incumbent, the structure diff, the delta wins throughout).

## Honest scope
Even this is not the *final* §10 verdict: one Tier-1 app family, **now plus two real Tier-2 apps
(directional, N=3)**, cl100k for Tier-1 / Gemini for Tier-2 (not every deployment tokenizer),
**and the delta's own named stress case — a keyed-list REORDER — is
not yet run** (TodoMVC has no native reorder). So the 30/30 precision AND recall covers the
four non-stress quadrants only (insert / attr-flip / removal / navigation); the
`structural-diff.ts` incumbent is reorder-safe by construction, but absent a reorder
interaction that fairness is unexercised here. It removes the author-bias objection and adds
the navigation + a reorder-safe diff + recall/precision + statistical rigor #23 lacked. The
remaining gaps are enumerated above and in #25.
