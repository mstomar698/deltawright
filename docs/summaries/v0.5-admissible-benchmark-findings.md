## §10 assessment (on real third-party code)

**(a) token win — HOLDS on code we didn't write.**
- **Vs re-snapshot** (the MCP default): **0.19–0.45×** on every interaction — the delta is
  2–5× smaller than re-dumping the a11y tree, even on a small app.
- **Vs a STRUCTURE-AWARE diff at information parity** (the strongest fair incumbent —
  reorder-safe, minimal): delta-lite is **0.27–0.69×** on toggle/delete/filter-nav and
  **~tied (1.03)** on add. Honest: against a *good* diff of a tiny insert, the delta can't
  win much (both are ~30 tokens); its edge grows for removals (0.27) and attribute flips.
- The full delta additionally carries geometry + actionability the diff structurally cannot.

**(b) noise — not exercised here (by design).** TodoMVC has no background churn, so this
run isolates *author bias*, not noise. And the coalescer is **exact** on real third-party
DOM: reported `nodes` = 2–4 = precisely the change scope (new `<li>` + footer count;
removed `<li>` + count; the flipped item; the filtered-out items). No over-reporting. The
noise stress remains #23's synthetic finding → causal attribution (#15).

**(c) settle — PERFECT on the real quiet app.** `capped 0/30` on every interaction
(including the **navigation**) — settle quiesced cleanly every time.

**Recall AND precision — 30/30 on all four interactions, both frameworks.** The delta
captured the primary intended change in *every* trial (recall), including the hash-route
navigation (it reported the filtered-out items as removed) — and every node it reported was
an expected consequence (precision): zero over-report against the hand-labeled change set. It
never missed a change and never padded one.

**TIME — the one real, honest regression, confirmed on real UI.** The delta is
**~7–10× slower** than snapshot+diff on add/toggle/delete (1.4–1.7 s vs 0.14–0.22 s). Root
cause found: TodoMVC's `.destroy` button is `opacity:0` until hover, so it is *correctly*
NOT-actionable — but the trial-probe waits the full `trialTimeoutMs` (1200 ms) to determine
that. So a real hover-reveal control costs a full timeout. filter-nav is fast (215 ms)
because its changes are removals + already-actionable links (no slow probe). The verdict is
*right*; the cost is the O(nodes) serial trial with a full-timeout floor → async/bounded
reconciliation + a smarter not-actionable short-circuit (#18).

> **Update — #18 landed.** Reconciliation is now bounded-concurrent (each node still gets
> its full authoritative trial; verdicts + recall unchanged, 30/30). The reconcile step is
> ~8× faster on many-node deltas; on this small app the gain is minor (few nodes, one slow
> probe sets the floor), but on a 305-node delta the overall action dropped 12.3 s → 3.4 s.

**Overall: the author-bias objection is removed and the core bet HOLDS on real third-party
code** — a consistent token win over re-snapshot, competitive-to-better vs a structure-aware
diff at parity, exact coalescing, reliable settle, and perfect recall including on a
navigation. The single confirmed weakness is the actionability *time* cost, which real
hover-reveal UIs make concrete (#18). This is a materially stronger §10 signal than #23 —
though still one app, one framework, quiet, cl100k (not Claude). The remaining gaps are #25's
open items.
